# Voice

How dogsled.dev sounds. Match this whenever you write copy for any dogsled surface — landing page, blog post titles, email signatures, OG card text, social posts.

---

## Core principle

Direct, builder-grade, finance-literate. **Specific over general. Particular over performative.** No "thought leadership." No buzzwords. Just the work.

A reader who's been a CFO for 20 years should think *"someone who knows what they're doing wrote this."* A reader who builds software should think *"someone who actually ships wrote this."* Hit both.

---

## Brand line (canonical)

> **Aim high. Pull hard. Leave tracks.**

Three sentences, three periods. Periods matter — they imply *then*, not *and*. Past, present, future.

Variations only when necessary:
- "Aim high. Pull hard. *Leave tracks.*" (italic on closing verb-phrase)
- "Aim high — pull hard — leave tracks." (em-dashes for inline body copy use)

**Never:** "Aim higher, pull harder, leave better tracks." (Bastardization. The line is locked.)

---

## Say (good examples)

These are the rhythm and register dogsled uses:

- "A builder's log for modern finance."
- "Pulls weight. Doesn't bark."
- "Built it, shipped it, here's the post."
- "Spreadsheets are tools — not the answer."
- "Lead dog. Long haul."
- "Forecasts that don't break. Dashboards that breathe."
- "Field notes from the frontier of FP&A."
- "Small, fast tools for back-office finance."

What these have in common: short, specific, declarative. Often two-clause sentences with a strong period in the middle. They sound like *someone said them*, not like marketing department-by-committee.

---

## Don't say (kill on sight)

These trigger an immediate rewrite:

- ❌ "Synergies"
- ❌ "Unlocks" (as a noun: *"this unlocks new opportunities"*)
- ❌ "Verticals"
- ❌ "Disrupting"
- ❌ "Revolutionizing"
- ❌ "Best-in-class"
- ❌ "Cutting-edge" / "Bleeding-edge"
- ❌ "Leveraging AI-powered solutions"
- ❌ "End-to-end transformation"
- ❌ "Reach out to learn more"
- ❌ "Empowering teams to..."
- ❌ "Elevate your..."
- ❌ "Unleash..."
- ❌ "Game-changing"
- ❌ "Take your X to the next level"

These all signal *"this was written to sound impressive."* dogsled writes to sound *honest.*

---

## Italic discipline

Italic is a flourish, not a default. **One italic phrase per heading, maximum.**

Italic should land on the *idea word* — the word the sentence is really about. Always pair with `--ember` color.

**Good:**
- "A builder's log for *modern finance.*"
- "The DataStore is the *swap point.*"
- "What spreadsheets are *actually* for."

**Bad:**
- "A *builder's log* for *modern finance.*" (two italics — pick one)
- "*A builder's log for modern finance.*" (italicizing the whole sentence — that's just italic text, not a flourish)
- "Why *FP&A* needs *modern tools.*" (italicizing acronyms is weird)

---

## Periods, not commas

The brand line uses periods between actions for a reason. Each beat is its own sentence — past, present, future, separate stages. Carry that discipline into other writing.

**Prefer short declarative sentences:**

> *"Built it. Shipped it. Here's the post."*

over

> *"I built it and shipped it, and here's a post about it."*

The first sounds like dogsled. The second sounds like everyone else.

---

## Status pill copy

When using the pill system in copy, keep the vocabulary consistent:

- **SHIPPING** — a thing that's live and commercial
- **ACTIVE** — a thing being built right now
- **NOTES** — a draft of something
- **GRADUATED** — a thing that's done, archived, earned its slot

If you need a new status state, *don't.* Pick the closest match from the four. The system's value is its constraint.

---

## Anti-patterns specific to AI-generated brand copy

These are the tells that out a generated post or page. Avoid them:

- ❌ "In today's fast-paced world..." — no.
- ❌ "Whether you're X or Y, this is for you" — generic catch-all
- ❌ Three-item bulleted lists with parallel verb-led phrasing for everything
- ❌ Em-dash between every clause in body copy
- ❌ "It's not just X — it's Y" framing
- ❌ Opening with a rhetorical question ("Ever wondered why...")
- ❌ Closing with "the future of X is here"
- ❌ Phrases like "powered by", "driven by", "fueled by" applied to brand promises

If your draft uses any of these, rewrite it.

---

## Voice for client-facing work

When dogsled is delivering for a client (not writing as itself), shift register slightly:

- Stay direct, but more formal
- Drop the surfer-casual energy
- Keep the specificity, lose the swagger
- "Pulls weight. Doesn't bark." → not for client deliverables; "Built for X firm. Maintained by Chris." → fine

---

## When in doubt

Read it out loud. If you'd cringe saying it to a CFO over coffee, rewrite it. If you'd say it in a Slack message to a builder friend, ship it.
